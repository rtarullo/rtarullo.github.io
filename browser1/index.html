US Women's National Team Wins World Cup

PQ MONTHLY - The U.S. Women’s National Soccer Team (USWNT) crushed defending champion Japan in the FIFA Women’s World Cup (WWC) finals Sunday in Vancouver, B.C. 5-2. In addition to the match being a blowout, with four of the five U.S. goals scored within the first 17 minutes, it also went down as a WWC record for the highest points scored in a final match—totaling 7.

Carli Lloyd, our nation’s new Wonder Woman of soccer, was the first to score in this match and secured the fastest hat trick in World Cup history, clinching the “Golden Ball” best player of the tournament award. Her last goal was launched—seemingly from midfield—and landed unbelievably just over Japan’s goalie Ayumi Kaihori, slamming the door on any question of which team came to play and win.

When the game ended trophies were handed out, awards were given and players ran ecstatically around the stadium with American flags flowing behind them as the crowd cheered. And Wambach immediately jumped up to kiss her wife in the stands, what was shown on the huge screen for the world to see. This display was met with an eruption of crowd-deafening applause. This embrace a hugely emotional moment for many watching, especially given the recent legalization of same-sex marriage in the U.S.—to have the image celebrated with such uplift and acceptance, the energy was palpable.
